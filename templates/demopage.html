Five of Toronto's Best Attractions

The CN Tower

The CN tower is the iconic landmark of Toronto, standing taller than all other buildings in toronto’s striking skyline. If you visit Toronto and you never ascended the CN tower, were you ever really here? Jokes aside, the view from the observation deck of the tower is amazing. If you have room in your budget to splurge on a meal, dining at the top of the tower at ‘360’ would surely be memorable.

Ontario Place

The Ontario Place is an iconic waterfront location. Previously closed, it has now been re-opened as a beautiful public space that is completely free to enter. During the winter, people are free to skate and take in the wonderful winter landscape.

Canada’s Wonderland

For those looking for some excitement on their visit to Toronto, this massive theme park is an excellent choice. Canada’s Wonderland is home to some of the most exhilarating rides, such as Behemoth and Leviathan. Though it is much further from downtown Toronto, anyone visiting will most certainly not regret the longer trip.

The ROM and The AGO

The Royal Ontario Museum and the Art Gallery of Ontario both house interesting displays of art, science and history. Both often have limited time exhibitions of different themes. Anyone who wants a slower day of learning and observation will surely enjoy given them a visit.

Kensington Market

Kensington Market is home to many local restaurants, bars, and stores. Strolling through the market will certainly bring you to just the meal you were craving, all while supporting smaller, local businesses.
